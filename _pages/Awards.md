Competitions
======
<div class="col-sm-9" style="display: flex; align-items: center; padding-left: 0px; text-align: justify;">
During my undergraduate studies, I participated in several high-level research-oriented academic competitions organized by the Urban Planning Society of China. In 2019, as a team leader, I collaborated with my teammates to win awards in both the social investigation and urban design categories. These competitions sharpened my skills in research, observation, analysis, and drawing, which provided essential support for my postgraduate studies.

    <div>
      <div class="title"><a href="/images/zzw2.png"><strong>Research Report on Comprehensive Urban and Rural Social Practice for Urban and Rural Planning Majors of Higher Education Institutions in China</strong></a></div>
    </div> 
      <div class="competition-description" style="display: flex; align-items: center; padding-left: 0px; text-align: justify;">
      <i>China’s urbanization strategy, with its focus on high-quality development, underscores the significance of “Opening and Sharing” in revitalizing public service facilities in large cities. Since 2018, Tianjin has utilized resources such as bank facilities to offer dining and rest services for outdoor workers, aiming to address social needs through shared spaces. However, these policy initiatives have shown uneven distribution over time and space, falling short of fully meeting workers’ needs. This research investigates a central urban worker port in Nankai District, Tianjin, analyzing the discrepancies between workers’ operational needs and the port’s spatial distribution and functions. Utilizing methods such as literature review, interviews, surveys, sentiment-semantic analysis, and network analysis, this study aims to propose recommendations for improving the planning and management of worker ports to enhance shared quality.</i>
    </div>
